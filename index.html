Introduction

E-wallets, also known as digital wallets, are reshaping how people manage their money and make transactions. From small retail purchases to paying utility bills, these technologies have become indispensable in the digital economy. With the rise of cashless societies, e-wallets are not just convenient tools, they represent a shift in consumer behavior, business operations, and financial systems.
E-wallets have revolutionized financial transactions in the Philippines, bridging gaps in financial inclusion and reshaping the economy. The COVID-19 pandemic accelerated their adoption, as consumers sought contactless payment solutions. At the same time, government initiatives, such as the Bangko Sentral ng Pilipinas’ (BSP) Digital Payments Transformation Roadmap 2020–2023, promoted a shift towards a cash-lite economy. These technologies have enhanced convenience and integrated millions into the digital financial ecosystem, making them critical for economic participation.
Recent advancements in mobile and financial technology have driven the rapid adoption of e-wallets worldwide. In the Philippines, this technological shift has proven particularly impactful, addressing long-standing challenges associated with financial inclusion, transaction inefficiencies, and economic participation. With a population exceeding 116 million (Worldometer, 2024), and a growing number of smartphone users, the Philippines offers a unique environment for the widespread adoption of e-wallets.

The adoption of e-wallets has been catalyzed by several factors, including the COVID-19 pandemic, which heightened the need for contactless payments, and government initiatives promoting a cash-lite economy. E-wallet platforms offer services such as money transfers, bill payments, and QR code transactions, all accessible through mobile devices.

Despite their advantages, e-wallets face challenges like cybersecurity risks, digital literacy gaps, and uneven internet access in rural areas. To ensure sustained growth, robust cybersecurity measures, and regulatory frameworks are essential for protecting users against fraud and data breaches. To keep e-wallets growing, it is important to improve online safety and create clear rules to protect users. Better internet access and teaching people how to use digital tools are also important to help more people benefit. As e-wallets grow, they could offer new features like small loans, investments, and insurance, becoming more useful for everyone. By working together, e-wallet companies and banks can help the Philippines lead in digital payments and create a more inclusive and modern economy.

Objectives:

  • To address how e-wallets simplify small transactions and enhance the speed of everyday purchases.

  • To discuss how e-wallets are contributing to the shift towards a cashless society and promoting financial inclusion.
  • To analyze potential hurdles in the adoption of e-wallets, such as security concerns, limited digital literacy, etc.

Review of Related Literature

The most observable rise of the use of e-wallets in the Filipino community started during the early months of the COVID-19 pandemic. E-wallets offered services that people needed most during this time: cashless; contactless; effortless. In this light, we collected and outlined different articles that will review our topics in different contexts.

The study conducted by Jimenez et.al. (2024), discussed the views of users regarding the patronization of e-wallets in the current phenomenon. The researchers concluded that customers frequently use e-wallets for financial transactions for a number of reasons, particularly: the effortless interface, convenience of access, health safety– in terms of the prevention of disease transmission, and the authority it gives them in managing their accounts and transactions. Olipane and Inocencio (2023) investigated the perception of customers towards digital payment as to E-Wallet, Mobile Banking and Debit/Credit card in relation to the level of their convenience. Their study discovered that users deem e-wallets as most useful and convenient among other modes of digital payment. In areas like convenience, accessibility, and security, e-wallets provide more preferable service and had the highest ratings from the respondents.

A study that utilized the unified theory of acceptance and use of technology (UTAUT) was conducted in 2021, to explore its manifestations in the current generation's adoption of e-wallets. It examined how areas, mainly: perceived usefulness, perceived ease of use, social influence, facilitating condition, lifestyle compatibility, and perceived trust, influence the research topic. UTAUT suggests that the actual use of technology is determined by behavioural intention (Venkatesh et al., 2003, as cited in Marikyan & Papagiannidis 2023). This theory considers performance expectancy, effort expectancy, social influence, and facilitating conditions as the four main constructs dependent on use of technology. Yang et. al. applied this theory and found its relevance to the intention and adoption of e-wallets in Malaysia. The study was not able to observe the effects of age, gender and education on the phenomenon.
